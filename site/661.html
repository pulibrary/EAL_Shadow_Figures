description
Commander in Chief Palace property. Ming or Qing style hall seen from ¾ view. Six hinged pieces. Green roof tiles, roof beam shows five blue flower scenes; green monster faces bite beam at either end, scaled bodies curve above their heads; green, red faces at all roof ends; corner ornaments of 3 fox-dogs, 1 symmetrical object above hanging bell; green tile ends; red, green patterned under eaves; black threshold and 3 panels of latticework window; outside, four red columns flank entrance. Inside, red table with straight legs, lotus water deco front panel; partial view of red chair covered with tiger skin. On table, white bundle with red ‘x’, short knife bundle on stand, red fringed green triangular flags, pair vessels holding red tipped green sticks, small cup, small black block. Bldg exterior reddish with lower portion blue brickwork; surrounding stone balustrade opens to stairs, black lacquer stands holding 5 red, long handled weapons and flag flank stairs on either side. 2 metal pins stuck in upper extremes for scrim attachment.
dimensions
H: 33 ¾ x 30 ½; column 11 ¼ x 1; table 8 x 5 ½; weapon 10.
